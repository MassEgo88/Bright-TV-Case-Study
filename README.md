Project Overview

This project presents a data-driven analysis of Bright TV's content consumption patterns across South Africa. The goal is to understand how users engage with the platform, identify periods and regions of low engagement, and propose actionable strategies to increase consumption and grow the user base sustainably.

---

Objectives

- Analyse overall consumption trends by day, time, and region
- Understand user behaviour across age, gender, and demographic segments
- Identify low-consumption gaps and underserved markets
- Recommend strategies to increase engagement and platform growth

Key Findings

Consumption Trends
- Viewership spikes on Fridays and dips on Tuesdays and Wednesdays
- Weekdays account for ~60% of total consumption, weekends for ~40%
- Prime time and afternoons are peak viewing windows — highest opportunity for targeted engagement

 User Behaviour
- The 25–34 age group drives the most engagement (~50% of consumption)
- The 18–24 age group follows, primarily consuming youth-oriented content
- Male users account for 86% of total platform engagement vs. 12% female

Regional Insights
- Gauteng dominates consumption across both weekdays and weekends
- North West, Free State, Northern Cape, and Unknown regions show the lowest engagement — representing the biggest growth opportunities

Low Consumption Channels
- Female users consistently engage less across all channels
- Several channels show minimal female viewership — indicating a content gap

---

Recommendations

 Increasing Engagement on Low Days
1. Content Scheduling — Drop premium or trending episodes on low-activity days (e.g. Tuesdays)
2. Push Notifications — Send targeted reminders during low-consumption periods
3. Personalised Recommendations — Suggest content based on individual viewing history
4. Gamification — Introduce watch streaks, badges, and reward systems
5. Promotions — Offer free data hours or discounted streaming windows during off-peak times

---

Growth Strategy

+ Acquire New Users
- Telecom Partnerships — Bundle data + streaming with mobile providers
- Device Pre-installs — Get Bright TV pre-loaded on affordable smartphones
- Referral Programs — Reward users for bringing friends onto the platform
- Local Content Investment — Produce regional and local shows to drive cultural connection
- Social Media Campaigns — Use short-form clips on TikTok, Instagram, and Facebook to drive traffic

Retain & Grow Existing Users
- Improved Onboarding — Personalise the first-day experience with smart recommendations
- AI-Driven Suggestions — Implement "Because you watched…" style content discovery
- Habit Building — Develop daily/weekly show schedules to encourage routine viewing

---

Conclusion

Bright TV's consumption is shaped by time of day, content type, and user habits. The biggest untapped opportunity lies in low-consumption days and underserved provinces. Growth will come from smarter engagement strategies, stronger acquisition channels, and a content approach that speaks to a broader South African audience.
